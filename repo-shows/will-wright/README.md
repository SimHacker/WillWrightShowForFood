## What this is

A **Repo Show**: a collaborative, GitHub-native, live conversation whose
artifact and stage is *this repo*, and which **follows through to working
code**. Part of **Micropolis Class** — the open methodology of going to the
people who actually invented this stuff, capturing their credited ideas in
public, and letting AI implement them. The product is the methodology + the
knowledge; the game is the proof. *Show, don't tell.*

## Why topic-less

Will contains multitudes. Rather than box the first conversation into one
subject, we open the door wide: **talk about anything he wants.** The threads
he pulls become the season — each one a future show. (See `episodes.yml`.)

## What's in it for Will

- Reify and build on his legacy — the open-source descendant of his own game,
  done right, with him as credited co-designer.
- A warm, low-pressure platform to talk about **Proxi** and whatever he's into
  now — real interest generated among players, investors, and media.
- His ideas **implemented live, for free** — not just discussed. Most designers
  rarely get to *see* their offhand ideas built; here they get built on stream.
- No prep, no homework, no assigned topic. Just a great conversation with an
  old colleague. (Don worked with Will on The Sims; Will's 1996 Stanford talk
  is what pulled Don to Maxis in the first place.)
