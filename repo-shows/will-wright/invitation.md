

# Draft: Invitation to Will Wright

**Subject:** A Repo Show with you, built around your 1996 Dollhouse talk

---

Hey Will,

Great talking yesterday — a lot whizzed by on the phone, so let me put the gist
in writing here. That way we don't have to cram any of it into the show itself
(the show stays loose); this is just so the stuff that flew past has somewhere
to land.

First, the spark: a while back I dug Stanford's recording of your 1996 talk to
Terry Winograd's class out of the archives, put it up on YouTube, and cleaned up
the transcript. Watching it again knocked me out — it's the talk where a student
asked what you were working on, you said "Oh, God…", and then loaded a SimCity
file into **Dollhouse** and gave an early public preview of what would become
The Sims. That talk is the reason I went to Maxis. I've been wanting to do
something with it, and here it is.

I'm starting a thing I'm calling a **Repo Show** — like a blog is a web log, a
Repo Show is a show whose whole stage is a public GitHub repo you can clone 
and build on. The idea is the opposite of AI slop: instead of letting a model
regurgitate a flattened echo of people's work, I go straight to the people who
*actually invented this stuff*, we talk in public, and the AI does the part
it's good at — turning the ideas into working code, live. **Show, don't tell.**

**I'd love you to be the first guest — and there's no assigned topic. You're the
topic.** We'd just start from that 1996 video and follow wherever you want to go.
Two things I think would be fun:

- **The arc writes itself.** In 1996 you did post-mortems of three games — SimAnt
too simple, SimEarth too complex, SimCity 2000 just right — and then pivoted to
*preview* The Sims. Thirty years after that preview, and twenty-six years after
The Sims shipped, we can finally do the **post-mortem on The Sims 1** — and go
through your own words from back then: what you nailed
(Simlish as Peanuts "mwa-mwa" adult-talk, plug-in objects that "advertise,"
user content posted to the net, storytelling…) and what you'd call differently 
now.
- **The crown jewel.** The thing you nailed in '96 that *still* isn't really
built is **data portability** — characters, homes, objects, memories moving
between games and universes (your train-set hobby model). I want to actually
build it with you, on the show: a little Proxi ↔ Sims bridge. Your idea,
finally running.
- **Reincarnate Slats.** Remember **Slats** — the robot brain I programmed at
  your **Stupid Fun Club**, who showed up in the One Minute Movies (*Servitude*,
  *Empathy*)? I'm pretty sure I still have all the code (and a Windows laptop
  that might run it — emulation if not). I'd love to make **bringing Slats back**
  a quest we work through on the show: read the old code together, figure out
  how to revive him, have him **call into the chat**, interview him, reprogram
  him, interview him again. **RoboResurrection** — Stupid Fun Club energy,
  constructionism as theater. Totally optional, but I think you'd get a kick out
  of it. Seed doc: `repo-shows/will-wright/slats-reincarnation.yml`.

**The bigger picture (the stuff that whizzed by on the call).** None of this has
to make it into the first show — it's just the mission the show is part of:

1. **Bridging characters between worlds.** Your 1996 dream of data portability,
  for real: characters, homes, objects, and memories moving between games and
   universes — **Proxi ↔ The Sims ↔ Stardew Valley** and beyond, picking up a
   little Timothy-Leary-Mind-Mirror personality read along the way. Your soul-file
   travels; each world re-renders it in its own medium.
2. **A web-browser platform for Sims tools.** I'm rebuilding the whole Sims
  content stack — rendering, reading, writing, editing, *generating* — as a
   reimagined, open platform for building content tools that **run right in the
   browser** (Transmogrifier, RugOMatic, the WigOMatic… reborn, web-native).
3. **Reviving the Sims Exchange.** Now that The Sims 1 is on Steam (Legacy
  Collection) — but **without** the Exchange — I want to rebuild that community
   save-file and story-sharing site. The missing piece that made The Sims a
   *medium*, brought back for the people who still play.

What's in it for you, honestly: it builds on your legacy instead of strip-mining
it; it's a friendly place to talk about **Proxi** and whatever you're into now;
your offhand "what if it could…" ideas get implemented on the spot (you rarely
get to *see* that); and there's **zero homework** — no prep, no slides, I do all
the setup.

Format: live (thinking Twitch), announced ahead, and the audience actually
participates — they bring questions and even contribute to the repo. It's warm
and earnest — Phil-Donahue-working-the-room, not gotcha-podcast. I run the whole
thing in Cursor, screen-shared, so people watch the ideas become real.

All of it is **open to discussion** — the framing, what we record, what's public.
Nothing happens without your say-so, and anything beyond the conversation itself
we'd sort out separately.

No rush, no pressure. If it sounds fun, poke around what I've been cooking:

**https://github.com/SimHacker/WillWrightShowForFood**

You can read it in a browser without a GitHub account — start at the README. We'll find a time.

It would mean the world to do the first one with you.

— Don
