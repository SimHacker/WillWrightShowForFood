## Outside vs inside

Don's framing (Sep 2013):

> Charles told me the "inside" story from his perspective at EA, how he was deputized to shepherd it through after Will got the ball rolling, and how he called in some favors he'd done for people to make it happen.

Charles pushed back — it was **not** a quid-pro-quo trade:

> After a year of pushing it, [the paperwork] still needed to get signed or it wouldn't happen. The officer I went to was someone I had been on really good terms with since I helped land the BP deal. He made getting this doc signed a priority. … He finally took it to **JR (the CEO)** and advocated for it.

Key constraints Charles named:

- **No revenue** attached → corporate resistance ("time is never free")
- Risks were low but **non-zero** — needed an officer willing to put their name on it
- Will started the ball; Charles **shepherded** contracts and signatures internally

Don's reply (Sep 2013):

> Somebody actually has to have permission (and a mandate) to spend time on it … Thanks a lot for doing that!

This complements the Nov–Dec 2006 timeline in [simcity-open-source-saga](../simcity-open-source-saga/article.md): Will → Rod → Scott Evans → "Chuck, can you please run with this?" → DUX/Maxis contract archaeology in [simcity-unix-rights-history](../simcity-unix-rights-history/article.md).
