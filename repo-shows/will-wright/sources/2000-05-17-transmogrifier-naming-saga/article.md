# Why "Transmogrifier"? — the naming saga (May 2000)

*Harvested from Don Hopkins archival paste (`big-prompts.txt`, May 2000 mail). Republished in [WillWrightShowForFood](https://github.com/SimHacker/WillWrightShowForFood).*

---

## Showstopper: EA Legal red flag

After Don shipped SimTransmogrifier documentation and the program, the first problem EA found was a **showstopper**:

> Somebody in EA Legal raised a red flag about the name **"Transmogrifier"**! Time to start paying the lawyers to come up with a new name.

---

## Chris Baena (Maxis) — remove the name

> We are most definitely **not** using the name Transmogrifier due to copyright issues. Could you please **remove that name from your website ASAP** and put in a placeholder generic name until we get word back from our legal department.

---

## Don's defense (dictionary + PKD + anagrams)

Don argued the word is generic (Merriam-Webster dates it to **1656**, "origin unknown"), cited PKD's *Transmigration* (not Transmogrification), Piers Anthony, and noted `transmogrifier.com` domains were taken.

Technical objection: renaming means editing Visual C++ project files, `.dsp`, class wizard, docs, screenshots.

**Anagram server output** (selection):

- "Grin O'er Sim Fart"  
- "Frontier Sim Rag"  
- "I Erg Sim Fortran"  
- "Granite from IRS"  

> If you're 18 or older and don't mind fart jokes, click here for even more funny Transmogrifier anagrams!

Chris's counter: **Calvin and Hobbes** — "It was Calvin's gun I think."

---

## Will Wright — lawyers stand down

> After looking around on the web (and consulting my Webster's dictionary) I believe Don is right.  
> I didn't realize that this was a **real word** (and one that has been around since around 1650).  
> I also found a bunch of shareware and freeware things that used the name so I think there's **no problem** with us using it.

The tool kept the name. Site: `thesimstransmogrifier.com`.
