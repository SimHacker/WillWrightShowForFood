## Why Maxis in 1997?

I left Interval Research because Will invited me to work on **The Sims** — the Project X / Dollhouse demo had the depth I wanted: user-created stories, objects, characters, and a simulation you could see inside. The SimCity Unix ports were my own obsession; The Sims was the team’s.

## EA transition

Maxis went public, then EA acquired us mid-development. Culturally we stayed a small team in San Mateo with Will’s design voice; legally we were EA. The crunch to ship The Sims under EA marketing was real, but the core team culture stayed casual and craft-focused compared to typical EA studios.

## Pie menus

CHI'88 experiment with Ben Shneiderman, Mark Weiser, Jack Callahan — circular menus faster and less error-prone than linear menus. I brought them to NeWS, SimCity, The Sims, and everything since. Reference: radial menus in older HCI literature plus our published paper.

## Work week

Heavy implementation time; Will and designers iterated constantly. Steering-committee-style demos; lots of programming, tool-building (MaxScript pipeline, Transmogrifier path), and playing builds to break them. Less "meetings" than "show what you built."

## Did I play The Sims 1?

Yes — testing, but also for fun with weird edge cases. Shipping burns you out, but the emergent stories kept it interesting.

## Proudest / wish I'd done better

**Proud:** Character animation pipeline with Ocean Quigley; **SimShow** and **Transmogrifier** before/after ship; pie menus; inclusion work ("Mother Theresa to everyone else" scale); Motive.c lineage with Will.

**Better:** More SimAntics in players' hands earlier; Edith tooling surfaced sooner; even more documentation (which is why I'm publishing design docs now).

## Sims legacy today

The UCC ecosystem we seeded (Exchange, fan sites, SimFreaks/SimSlice lineage) mattered as much as the box. Micropolis Home is that federation idea with modern git + web tools.

## Biggest focus pre-ship (1999–2000)

Tools, UI, shipping content pipeline, fan tools (SimShow), stability, and making the game moddable without breaking it.

## Tone shift (sitcom → less sitcom)

Design moved from pure sitcom toward player-authored drama — Will's dollhouse / TV metaphor evolved as we saw what players actually did in prototypes.

## Marketing vs game

EA marketing was EA; we fought for the game we built. Pre-launch fan tools helped set honest expectations.

## After Maxis

Maxis taught me player tools, pipelines, and shipping inclusive sims — carried to Connected Media, Stupid Fun Club robots, OpenLaszlo, OLPC Micropolis, and now Micropolis Home.
