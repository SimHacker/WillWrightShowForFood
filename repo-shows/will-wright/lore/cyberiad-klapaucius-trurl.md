# Klapaucius & Trurl — the Cyberiad thread (Will & Don as Lem's constructor robots)
# Surface EARLY, while discussing SimCity. Pull Palm in. Public lore for the show.

> *"You ask me whether they really suffer, or only seem to? ... A being that behaves exactly as
> a suffering being behaves IS a suffering being."* — the heart of the argument, in spirit.

## The pairing

Will Wright's longtime alias is **Klapaucius**. It's also the **original *The Sims 1* money cheat**
(later changed to `rosebud`). Both come from **Stanisław Lem's *The Cyberiad*** (1965) — a comic
cycle about two genius **constructor robots**, **Trurl** and **Klapaucius**, who build impossible
machines and argue about what they've made.

So: **Don nicknames himself "Trurl"** — pairing with Will "Klapaucius." Two world-builders, decades
of friendship, arguing fondly about their creations. (The same way Will nicknamed himself
Klapaucius, Don takes Trurl — it frames us as a matched set.)

## The tale that rhymes with SimCity

**"The Seventh Sally, or How Trurl's Own Perfection Led to No Good."** Trurl builds a deposed
tyrant a tiny **simulated kingdom** in a box — miniature subjects, cities, armies — so the tyrant
can rule (and oppress) to his heart's content without harming anyone real. Klapaucius is horrified:
he argues the little simulated beings **suffer for real** — that behaving exactly like suffering
beings *is* suffering, regardless of scale or substrate.

That argument — **do our simulated people really feel?** — is the moral engine under SimCity, The
Sims, and every microworld Will ever built. It's the perfect thread to pull **early**, while
discussing SimCity, before the 1996 talk.
