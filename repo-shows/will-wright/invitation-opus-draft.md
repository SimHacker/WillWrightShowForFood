Hey Will,

Quick follow-up to yesterday — there's one thread I want to pull on, because I think it's
right in your wheelhouse and it got me genuinely excited.

You know the thing you nailed in the 1996 Dollhouse talk that *still* nobody's actually
built? **Data portability between games** — your train-set hobby model, characters and
homes and objects and memories traveling between worlds. I want to build a real
**Proxi ⇔ Simopolis ⇔ The Sims** bridge with you, on the show. Both ends are *your* games;
Simopolis (the open Micropolis descendant) is just the waystation in the middle.

Here's the part I think you'll like. The motivation isn't "wouldn't it be neat if files
moved between games." It's deeper and it's *yours*: **The Sims was always about making a
character that represents YOU.** But you don't only play The Sims — you play a dozen games.
So the thing you'd actually want is one **über-character that represents you across every
game you play**, that travels through all of them, picking up abilities in each, and *syncs
back*. Your real self isn't siloed per-game; why should your soul-file be?

The model I've been sketching for how that works is **serial endosymbiosis** — Lynn
Margulis's thing. A cell engulfs a free-living organism and keeps it as an organelle that
*retains its own DNA and runs in parallel* — mitochondria for respiration, then later
chloroplasts for photosynthesis, each acquisition adding a new capability without erasing
what came before. Your über-character is the cell; **each game it passes through gets
engulfed and kept as a nested-membrane organelle** — a little sub-world inside the character
with its own attributes intact, all of them living in parallel. Import from Proxi, you grow
a Proxi organelle. Round-trip through The Sims, the Sims organelle evolves. The *whole shape*
stays stable; only the sub-world you just visited changes. Characters inside characters,
membranes inside membranes — and the soul-file is the source of truth, so you can send a
character off to die in some dungeon and regenerate it at will. Non-destructive, regenerable,
parallel.

(The part that made me laugh when it clicked: Margulis co-authored the **Gaia hypothesis**
with **James Lovelock** — your SimEarth guy. The biology behind this character model loops
right back to SimEarth's own science advisor. Felt like the universe nodding.)

And here's the twist I *really* want to show you: we treat **Timothy Leary's "Mind Mirror"**
as just another world to bridge to — except instead of reimplementing it, we run the **actual
1986 EA binary in an Apple ][ emulator** right in the browser. So your Sims character uploads
to Simopolis, downloads *into Mind Mirror*, gets configured and psychoanalyzed in the real
40-year-old game, and uploads back out — changed. Its Mind Mirror reading lives in its own
membrane (its own organelle), and on sync those personality dimensions **diffuse** into the
sibling organelles that can map them — nudging the Sims traits, the Proxi model — like
properties seeping across a semi-permeable membrane, never overwriting. (Leary's Mind Mirror
was EA-published too — all in the family.)

On the show we'd do the smallest, funniest complete version of it live — I've got a
candidate ("Wig-O-Matic," don't ask, it involves dogs in bouffants) — a single object with a
soul-file that any world can wear, reinterpret, edit, and keep in sync. The tiniest possible
proof of the crown jewel.

Now let me draw the *whole* picture, because it's bigger than one bridge and I think you'll
see your own fingerprints all over it. Picture a powers-of-ten zoom — and at every level
there's already a Maxis game sitting there waiting. At the **home** scale it's **The Sims** —
and notice that at that scale there's barely any *simulation* at all; it's really about
**content and dreaming** (characters, stories, objects), which is exactly where an LLM
belongs now. Zoom out to the **city** and it's **SimCity** (that's literally Micropolis, the
open one I've got running). Out again to the **planet** and it's **SimEarth** — Gaia, climate,
ecosystems — which loops *right back* to the endosymbiosis idea, because Lovelock's Gaia and
Margulis's biology are the same intellectual family. Out again to the **cosmos** and it's
**Spore** — creatures to civilizations to space — and Spore even has its *own* internal
powers-of-ten ladder (cell, creature, tribe, civ, space). And cutting *across* all of those
zoom levels is **Proxi** — because Proxi is the part that models the *character*, the *you*,
with AI. The levels are the worlds; Proxi is the soul that moves through them. It even works
*sideways*, across **versions** — a character round-tripping Sims 1 ⇄ 2 ⇄ 3 ⇄ 4, each kept
as its own faithful little world. So the zoom is almost three-dimensional: across scales,
across a single game's own levels, and across versions.

And here's the thing — **these are mostly YOUR games.** You don't need a federation of
strangers to make cross-game characters real; you made a whole universe of compatible worlds
yourself. It's the natural place to start. This is really just a continuation of what
*Streets of SimCity* already did — let you drive around inside your own SimCity save. You
said it out loud in 1996: data should move between games. I've been chewing on it ever since,
and clearly so have you (that's *Proxi*). So let's actually **federate** them.
