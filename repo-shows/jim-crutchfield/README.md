# A Repo Show with Jim Crutchfield 🦋

> Edge of Chaos — video feedback, cellular automata, and the geometry of complexity. Recurse,
> digress, iterate.

**James P. Crutchfield** ("Chaos") — UC Davis Distinguished Professor of Physics, director of the
**Complexity Sciences Center**, SFI external faculty, and president of the **Art & Science
Laboratory** — pioneered **video feedback** as a dynamical system, the **"edge of chaos"** and
**evolving cellular automata to compute** (with Melanie Mitchell), and founded **computational
mechanics** (ε-machines). He was one of the UC Santa Cruz **Dynamical Systems Collective** behind
"Geometry from a Time Series" and the *Eudaemonic Pie* roulette computers.

## What we'd get into

- **Video feedback, live** — close the camera→screen→camera loop and steer spatiotemporal chaos.
- **Edge of chaos** — evolve cellular automata until they actually *compute*.
- **Geometry from a time series** — reconstruct a hidden attractor from one signal.
- **Computational mechanics** — ε-machines: the minimal model of a process's intrinsic computation.
- **A CA jam** with Don's **CAM6** platform, and (dream) [Scott Draves](../../characters/scott-draves/)'s
  generative art + [Norman Margolus](../../characters/norman-margolus/)'s **CAM-6** / reversible CA.
