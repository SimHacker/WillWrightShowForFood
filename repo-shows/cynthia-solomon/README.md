# A Repo Show with Cynthia Solomon 📼

> The turtle's treasure trove — Logo's history, narrated by one of its original designers.

**Cynthia Solomon** helped create **Logo** (BBN, 1967) and build the MIT Logo culture that gave us
turtle graphics and constructionist learning. On her
[YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@cynthiaso) she keeps a rare archive of early footage —
Papert, the Minskys, children at the turtle, the Atari Cambridge lab. This show is a guided
screening of that trove, narrated by someone who was there.

## What we'd get into

- **Guided screening** — clips from her channel, narrated live.
- **The birth of Logo** — BBN 1967 → the [MIT Logo Group](../../characters/seymour-papert/README.md) → the
  turtle → children as builders of their own understanding.
- **Atari Cambridge** — the children's computer that almost was.
- **Logo → today** — the through-line to **Snap!**, optionally with
  [Brian Harvey](../../characters/brian-harvey/README.md) and [Jens Mönig](../../characters/jens-monig/README.md).
