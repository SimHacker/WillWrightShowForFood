# A Repo Show with Scott McCloud 💭

> Understanding Comics → Understanding Simulations — closure, masking, and the imagination's role.

**Scott McCloud**'s *Understanding Comics* (1993) reframed the reader as an active participant:
**closure** fills the gap between panels, **masking** lets abstract characters live in detailed
worlds, and **amplification through simplification** makes a simple drawing carry big feeling. Those
ideas were a design bible for **The Sims**.

## What we'd get into

- **Closure & the gutter** — the imagination between the panels, and between the simulation's ticks.
- **Masking** — abstract Sims in detailed worlds; identification and empathy.
- **Amplification through simplification** — why sparse models invite rich imagination.
- **Understanding Simulations** — the comics lens applied to [The Sims](../../characters/will-wright/README.md)
  and Micropolis.
