## Who

**Owen Densmore** has spent 30+ years across **Xerox, Apple, and Sun Microsystems**. At Sun he created
the **object-oriented PostScript class system** — `class.ps` — for **NeWS**, the Network/extensible
Window System: he showed that **PostScript dictionaries alone** could implement a Smalltalk-style
class mechanism (inheritance, methods, instances) **without changing the interpreter** ("Object
Oriented Programming in NeWS," 1986; "A User-Interface Toolkit in Object-Oriented PostScript," with
**David Rosenthal**, 1987 — one toolkit earned a **patent in language design**).

Earlier, at **Apple**, he worked on Mac/Lisa hardware and software and the **Macintosh Printing
Architecture**. Later he was a **Chief Scientist at Sun** and led the **Java Car** concept work. After
the **Santa Fe Institute** Complex Systems Summer School he moved into **complexity science**,
co-founding the **Redfish Group** (agent-based modeling; a pillar of the NetLogo community).

## Why he's here (and a confession)

Don's own epithet — **"the User Interface Flower Child"** — is an **homage to Owen, the OG**. Don
shamelessly took the title. Owen is one of the great, kind elders of the **Silicon Valley Hippie
Mafia**, and core to the **NeWS gang** (Gosling, Rosenthal, van Hoff). His `class.ps` is the living
proof of the NeWS bet that runs through this whole repo: **send code, not commands** — PostScript for
code, data, *and* graphics. (See the trail: [`send-code-not-commands`](../../process/trails/send-code-not-commands.md).)
