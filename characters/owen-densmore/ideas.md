## What Owen has done

Owen Densmore — 30+ years across Xerox, Apple, and Sun Microsystems. At Sun he created the
object-oriented PostScript class system (`class.ps`) for NeWS (the Network/extensible Window
System) — showing that PostScript dictionaries alone could implement a Smalltalk-style class
mechanism (inheritance, methods, instances) with no changes to the interpreter ("Object
Oriented Programming in NeWS," 1986; "A User-Interface Toolkit in Object-Oriented PostScript," with
David Rosenthal, 1987 — one of the toolkits earned a patent in language design). Earlier, at
Apple, he worked on Mac/Lisa hardware+software and the Macintosh Printing Architecture;
later he was a Chief Scientist at Sun and led the Java Car concept work. After the
Santa Fe Institute Complex Systems Summer School (2000) he moved into complexity science and
co-founded the Redfish Group (agent-based modeling; a pillar of the NetLogo community).
