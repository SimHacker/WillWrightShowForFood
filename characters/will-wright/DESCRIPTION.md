# Will Wright — Description

👤 **Will Wright** is the game designer who redefined what a video game could be.
Co-founder of **Maxis**, he created **SimCity** (1989) and its siblings
**SimEarth**, **SimAnt**, and **SimCity 2000**, then **The Sims** (2000) — for
years the best-selling PC game ever — and the wildly ambitious **Spore** (2008).

His through-line is treating simulations as **toys rather than games**:
open-ended systems players explore to build their own understanding and tell
their own stories. He designed the simulation, the gameplay, the interface, and
the player's *mental model* as one coupled whole — the computer simulation being,
in his words, a *"compiler for the mental model."* From the distributed,
**object-centric AI** of The Sims ("the objects are all kind of advertising") to
the **Simulator Effect** (players imagine far more than is actually simulated) to
the **hobby model** of extensible, user-created content, his ideas reshaped
city-builders, life sims, and creative sandboxes ever since.

His work is deeply entwined with this project: Don Hopkins ported SimCity to Unix,
worked with Will on The Sims (1997–2000), and collaborated for years afterward at
the Stupid Fun Club and beyond. The whole "make the simulation understandable,
extensible, and playful" goal of Micropolis is an attempt to honor that vision.
