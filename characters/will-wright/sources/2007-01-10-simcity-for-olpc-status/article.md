# SimCity for OLPC — January 2007 status update

*Email from Don Hopkins to Rod Humble, Scott Evans, Charles Normann, and Will Wright — 10 January 2007. Harvest: [`simcity-for-olpc.txt`](../../../../DonHopkins/temp/old-email/simcity-for-olpc.txt).*

---

## Context

Three weeks after the December 2006 player-intro thread ([introducing the players](../2006-12-simcity-olpc-introducing-the-players/article.md)), Don pushes EA for a **binding relicensing decision** so he can apply the new license, ship a testable build, and hit the OLPC software freeze before mass production.

---

## Will Wright (quoted)

> "I support the idea wholeheartedly."  
> "I'd really like to see this happen, it seems like a very worthwhile project at no risk to us. Please let me know what I can do to move it forward if it hits a snag."

## Chain of custody (Don's summary)

- **DUX↔Don** and **DUX↔Maxis** contracts unearthed; copies given to Will; fax to EA on request  
- DUX contract **expired 2001**; §7(a): Maxis/EA retains rights in translated Unix versions  
- Proprietary dependencies stripped; port **"in the clear, unencumbered, and ready to go"**

## Technical status (Jan 2007)

- First-cut OLPC port **playable** — "without any bells or whistles"  
- Fedora Linux build with **multiplayer** support  
- Don had an **OLPC beta laptop**; offered VM image + source for **VMware on Windows**  
- Beta hardware generating positive press ([LinuxToday](http://www.linuxtoday.com/infrastructure/2007010902326NWHWEV), [BBC](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/technology/6246989.stm))

**Note:** Don referenced a private test tarball at `donhopkins.com/home/private_olpc/` — link-only in WWSFF; not mirrored in git.
