# Will Wright

**Simulation game designer** — co-founded **Maxis**, created **SimCity** (1989) and **The Sims**
(2000), and spent a career treating games as **toys and microworlds** rather than contests
with a single win state. This directory is a **public portrayal** for a Repo Show invitation —
polite, verifiable, and subject to Will's correction at any time. It is **not** Will Wright,
and we do not speak as him without consent.

> *"The player imagines more than we simulate."* — design tradition widely attributed to Will Wright (Simulator Effect)

## Why this repo exists around him

Don Hopkins and Will Wright share decades of history — Unix **SimCity**, **The Sims** at Maxis,
**Stupid Fun Club** robotics experiments, and a long-running conversation about **microworlds**
and **data portability** that surfaced publicly in Will's **1996 talk** to Terry Winograd's
Stanford class (the first public **Dollhouse** preview). That talk is why Don went to Maxis.

**Will Wright Show For Food** is the public apex of a Repo Show invitation: topic-less by design
(**Will is the topic**), anchored on that 1996 artifact, open to **Proxi**, **Slats**
RoboResurrection, and whatever threads Will wants to pull. See
[`repo-shows/will-wright/README.md`](../../repo-shows/will-wright/README.md).

## Design ideas (documented public traditions)

These are **about his published work**, not claims about private beliefs:

- **Toys, not games** — open-ended possibility spaces; players supply goals and stories.
- **Simulator Effect** — sparse simulation, rich player imagination filling the gaps.
- **Object-centric AI** — behavior advertised by objects (*"If you're hungry, eat me!"* — 1996 talk).
- **Hobby model** — extensible products and user-created content vs. blockbuster "movie model."
- **Implication over simulation** — show enough graphically; avoid number walls.
- **Data portability** — characters, homes, and worlds that could move between games (1996 goal; crown jewel for this project).
