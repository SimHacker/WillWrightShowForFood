## What Heinz has done

Co-author of **PIXIE** (1969) — radial menus on PDP-7/Titan with Neil E. Wiseman and John O. Hiles.
PhD on the PIXIE user interface. Decades leading **CARS** and computer assisted medicine / patient-
specific modelling. First-hand witness to Cambridge CAD/HCI dawn and US lab visits in the late 1960s.

## The hooks

### 0. David Chapman and the recovered films → *Flight of the Bumblebee*
Chapman at Cambridge University Library tracked down two 1969 PIXIE films, got them digitized,
and published them online — after Buxton's decade of broken promises. Don then cut the *Flight of
the PIXIE* edit (Yuja Wang's *Bumblebee*) to show how fast the radial menus were. Heinz is in the
footage. [Full arc](cambridge-films-flight-of-the-bumblebee.md).

### 0b. David Rosenthal — same PDP-7/Titan as Heinz
DSHR was at Cambridge ~1970 on the **same PDP-7/340 + Titan** stack; met Wiseman; saw PIXIE-era
demos. Don wanted DSHR to interview Heinz for CHM. Tie to NeWS reunion + networked-UI lineage.
[`../david-rosenthal/`](../david-rosenthal/)

### 1. Neil Wiseman — the person behind the citation
Lab culture, leadership, design judgment. Don't let Wiseman collapse into a paper header.

### 2. Cambridge zeitgeist (1967–1970)
PDP-7 + Type 340 display, light pen, Titan satellite link, distributed CAD (Rainbow group).

### 3. US research-lab trip + *Electronics* magazine (April 1969)
Who Heinz met, what systems he saw, what crossed the Atlantic both ways. Gilmore sent Don a scan
of the McGraw-Hill *Electronics* PIXIE article (young Heinz at the PDP-7) — Heinz wanted hi-res.
**Ask him to tell this story on air.**

### 4. PIXIE → CARS
Graphical man-machine communication for design → model-guided medicine today.

### 5. Preservation
Digitized films, *Flight of the PIXIE*, CHM oral history — honor Wiseman and Hiles through Heinz's voice.
