🐉📼 **Terry Winograd** — Stanford HCI professor and host of the legendary open public CS547 "People, Computers, and Design" seminar series (1990s). Each week a different luminary spoke plainly to students and the public, answered real questions, and left a VHS record. The direct forebear of the Repo Show format: one guest, open access, recorded for posterity, real back-and-forth.

Don attended multiple sessions (including Will Wright's April 26 1996 "Interfacing to Microworlds" — the Dollhouse demo that sent Don to Maxis — and James Gosling on Java). He later contacted Terry (2019–2023) about digitizing the archive, including the Will Wright talk (which Don personally uploaded, transcribed, and wrote about).

We call Terry "the dragon with the treasure trove of videos." We stand on his wide, generous shoulders — and on the shoulders of every great guest he hosted: Will Wright, James Gosling, S. Joy Mountford (HCI design leadership — *not* pie menus; that's Don's own work), Jef Raskin, Donald Norman, and dozens of others whose ideas are the common heritage the Repo Show exists to lift, implement, attribute, and teach.

The archive is the seed of a Connections-style series and the original Micropolis Class. Our job is to recover it, make it runnable, and pay it forward.
