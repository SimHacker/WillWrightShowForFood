## Who

**Peter Norvig** is Director of Research at Google and one of AI's most widely read teachers. With
Stuart Russell he wrote *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* — the textbook a large share of
the world's AI students learned from — and on his own he wrote *Paradigms of Artificial Intelligence
Programming* (PAIP, 1992), a deep, hands-on tour of classic AI in Common Lisp. Before Google he led
the Computational Sciences Division at NASA Ames. In 2011, with Sebastian Thrun, he taught a free
online AI course to roughly 160,000 students — one of the sparks of the modern MOOC era.

He is also an essayist whose short pieces have shaped programming culture:
[*Teach Yourself Programming in Ten Years*](https://norvig.com/21-days.html) (deliberate practice
beats "learn X in 24 hours" hype), [*How to Write a Spelling Corrector*](https://norvig.com/spell-correct.html),
and the *Gettysburg PowerPoint* parody.

Peter is a friend of Don's. He's **writing a book about AI** and has offered to include a **chapter
about what Don is doing** — which makes a Repo Show a perfect two-way fit: a recorded conversation
that's fun on its own and useful as source material for his chapter.

The topic is **his to choose** — [MOOLLM](https://github.com/SimHacker/moollm), skills, or AI in
general — and Don would love to bring in **Leela colleagues** like
[Henry Minsky](../henry-minsky/) and others. The *Ten Years* essay is a natural frame: what does
**deliberate practice** mean in an age of LLMs, and can *skills* be the constructionist artifacts we
practice with?
