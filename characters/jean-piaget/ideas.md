## What Jean has done

Jean Piaget (1896–1980) — Swiss psychologist and genetic epistemologist whose work transformed our understanding of how children think. By observing children closely (including his own), he described cognitive development as a sequence of stages (sensorimotor → preoperational → concrete operational → formal operational) and introduced schemas, assimilation, accommodation, and equilibration. His constructivism — the child as an active *builder* of knowledge — is the developmental bedrock beneath Seymour Papert's constructionism, and therefore beneath Logo, Scratch, OLPC, and this project's own learning mission. He founded the International Centre for Genetic Epistemology in Geneva (1955).
