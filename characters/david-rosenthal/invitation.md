## What I'd love to explore with you — your pick, any order, skip whatever tires you

### Your Cambridge ~1970 — same iron as PIXIE

- **PDP-7 + Type 340 + Titan** — what you actually used, who you worked with, what demos you saw.
- **Neil Wiseman** — from your perspective ~1970; how he comes across vs Heinz's memory from 1969.
- Lab culture, Rainbow group, distributed CAD — what felt normal then that looks revolutionary now.

### Heinz's segment — what only he can say

- The **1969 film** and paper — Heinz is *in* the footage; Chapman finally got it online in 2019.
- Wiseman and **John Hiles** as people, not citation headers.
- Heinz's US lab tour and the *Electronics* magazine photo — stories you may remember differently
  or complement.

### The lineage you uniquely bridge

- **PIXIE distributed GUI → Andrew → NeWS → X11/ICCCM** — not a lecture from me; your through-line.
- What carried from Cambridge satellite graphics into networked window systems?
- NeWS vs X11 — what you'd want historians to get right (you built both).

### Preservation — films, papers, emulators

- **LOCKSS** meets Chapman's film archive meets Lars booting emulators — one conversation.
- Lots of copies keep stuff safe — including 1969 telecine and Type 340 display lists.
- Runnable iron as preservation, not just PDFs and YouTube.

### Lars's segment — emulation you can react to

- **Type 340 Display Programming Manual** — vector refresh, display list, light-pen hits.
- What would *faithful* PDP-7/340 emulation need to demonstrate PIXIE-like interaction?
- Living hardware vs SIMH — what transfers, what's folklore.

### Optional later (separate episode)

- **Solo Repo Show** — NeWS reunion threads (Gosling, van Hoff, …), X11 architecture, Nvidia #4,
  Paul Evan Peters Award / LOCKSS deep dive. Seed: `repo-shows/david-rosenthal/`.
