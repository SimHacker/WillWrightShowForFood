🗄️ **David S. H. Rosenthal (DSHR)** — Co-developer of **NeWS** with James Gosling at Sun (co-author of *The NeWS Book*), and — uniquely — also a key architect of its winning rival **X11** (he wrote the **ICCCM** and holds an X security patent). Worked on **Andrew** at CMU with Gosling; **employee #4 and chief scientist at Nvidia**; then **Chief Scientist of LOCKSS** (Lots Of Copies Keep Stuff Safe) at Stanford Libraries — long-term **digital preservation** (2025 Paul Evan Peters Award). Born 1948, Cambridge UK.

Warm, mutually-respectful long-term friend of Don since the X10/X11/NeWS window-system wars. His 2024 "X at 40" posts and blog.dshr.org (*History of Window Systems*, *Pie Menus*, *Recreational Bugs*) surface Don's NeWS/pie-menu history. **Through-line:** PIXIE-era Cambridge → **Andrew** → **NeWS** → **X11/ICCCM**. At the **1985 Alvey Methodology of Window Management** workshop he and **Gosling published and joined the working-group discussions** (Andrew + SunDew papers; **Teitelman's** retrospective in the same proceedings — Teitelman later **Don's manager** on Sun's NeWS Toolkit team). [`window-systems-lineage.yml`](window-systems-lineage.yml)

**Co-guest on Heinz Lemke's PIXIE show** — same Cambridge **PDP-7/340 + Titan** ~1970; trio with **Lars Brinkhoff** (emulation). [`../heinz-lemke/invitation.md`](../heinz-lemke/invitation.md)

He's a **double-perfect** Repo Show guest: (1) the NeWS reunion (he can tell the honest story of why X beat NeWS), and (2) a **digital-preservation** show — his LOCKSS work and his "we're losing the battle to archive the web" warning are the dark twin of this project's "recover the treasure trove" mission. Bonus: his sharp, un-hyped blogging on AI's affordability crisis and AI slop is exactly the register this project wants. With Owen Densmore he even patented **OO in the Unix shell** — the lineage that runs straight into MOOLLM's directory-as-object. `contact: dshr@abitare.org`.
