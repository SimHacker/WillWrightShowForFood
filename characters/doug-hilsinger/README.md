## Who

**Doug Hilsinger** is a San Francisco Bay Area multi-instrumentalist. In Fall 2003 he recorded a
complete, **track-for-track cover of Brian Eno's *Taking Tiger Mountain (By Strategy)*** (1974)
with vocalist **Caroleen Beatty**, released in 2004 on **DBK Works** (CD #111) for the album's 30th
anniversary. The defining choice: he played **every instrument by hand** — drums, bass, guitar,
pedal steel, sitar guitar, glockenspiel, melodica, autoharp, percussion, treatments — with a
deliberate **ban on keyboards, synths, and drum machines**, while Beatty sang. Eno's chilly 1974
experiment came back **warm, organic, and aggressively alive**.

Made privately for love, the duo handed Eno a CD-R at his **Long Now Foundation** lecture in San
Francisco. Eno's reply — *"I am deeply moved by your versions of my songs. I like it very, very
much"* (and, to Pitchfork, *"They aren't bad songs after all"*) — is what brought it to release.

## Why he's here

Doug's record is the **living proof** of the principle under the whole Repo Show: you honor
something by **transforming** it, not copying it. Don crossed paths with Doug in San Francisco in
2006 and was given a copy of the CD — a story he loves and wants to tell properly.

Read it: [**Taking Tiger Mountain, by Serendipity**](taking-tiger-mountain-by-serendipity.md).

## The hope

Don has **reached out to Doug and not yet heard back** (as of 2026). The wish: sing Doug's praises,
share the story, play the work, and — if he's willing — have him join **Brian Eno**, **Scott
Draves**, and **Will Wright** to talk about honoring music by transforming it.
