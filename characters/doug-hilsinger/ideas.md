## What Doug has done

Doug Hilsinger is a San Francisco Bay Area multi-instrumentalist who, in Fall 2003,
recorded a complete, track-for-track cover of Brian Eno's *Taking Tiger Mountain (By
Strategy)* (1974) with vocalist Caroleen Beatty — released May 18, 2004 on DBK
Works (CD #111), subtitled *"A Modern Revolutionary Peking Opera"*; effectively a 30th-
anniversary tribute. Hilsinger played every instrument (drums, bass, guitar, pedal steel,
sitar guitar, glockenspiel, melodica, autoharp, percussion, background vox, treatments) with a
deliberate ban on keyboards, synths, samplers, and drum machines; Beatty sang and clapped.
Recorded at Saucefaucet Int'l (his practice space + bedroom), for love, never meant for retail.
The duo handed Eno a CD-R at his San Francisco lecture (Nov 2003) — and Eno phoned back:
*"I am deeply moved by your versions of my songs… I like it very, very much,"* thanked Doug for
making *"beautiful music"* from his *"old experiments,"* wrote the liner notes, and later
quipped to Pitchfork, *"They aren't bad songs after all."* That blessing brought the labor-of-
love tribute out into the world. Doug & Caroleen are SF scene veterans (both in Waycross;
Doug in BOMB + The Hall Flowers; Caroleen in The Bedlam Rovers, with Jon Langford of the
Mekons), and performed the album live as the ENORCHESTRA.
