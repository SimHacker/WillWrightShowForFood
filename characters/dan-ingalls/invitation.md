Dan —

We corresponded a few years back — Alan Kay introduced us around the **Fabrik** paper, and later
there was the **Pie Menus retrospective** thread and your patience with my UnityJS / Croquet /
Lively bridge notes. I'd like to turn that hallway conversation into a **Repo Show** — and I'd
also like to **warmly invite you** to help **remember Vanessa Freudenberg** with **Craig Latta**
and me.

**Remembering Vanessa (with Craig and me).**

Vanessa wrote **SqueakJS** — you credit it on the **[Smalltalk Zoo](https://smalltalkzoo.thechm.org/)**
welcome page. Craig's **Caffeine** builds on her VM; her **WASM insights** after SqueakJS are the
thread he carried forward. She was universally loved in the Squeak community. Memorial mode in this
repo: we *represent and discuss* her documented work and the stories friends tell — we never speak
as her. I'm inviting **Craig** for a live anchor episode and **Alan Kay** to join the memorial arc
if he's willing; your voice matters enormously — Zoo, Lively, the live-object lineage only you can
name properly on air. See [`../vanessa-freudenberg/memorial.md`](../vanessa-freudenberg/memorial.md)
and [`../../repo-shows/remembering-vanessa-freudenberg.yml`](../../repo-shows/remembering-vanessa-freudenberg.yml).

**What I'd especially like to explore with you at Ink & Switch**

You're building **Livelymerge** with Alex Warth — Smalltalk-like **liveness** on **Automerge**,
local-first mergeable live objects. That's the interesting problem for *right now*: how should
**data representation**, **organization**, **workflows**, and **collaboration** work when humans,
LLMs, **GitHub**, and a live stream share the same microworld?

Don's proposal to explore **with you** (not at you):

- **In-session** — Automerge / Livelymerge: mergeable live objects while the page runs
- **Published** — **YAML Jazz**: comments as a **semantically meaningful overlay** of intent and
  annotations — accessible and generatable by humans and LLMs; structure carries data, comments
  carry *why*
- **Durable** — **[GitHub-as-MMORPG](https://github.com/SimHacker/moollm/blob/main/designs/GITHUB-AS-MMORPG.md)**:
  branches as parallel timelines, PRs as merges, issues as quests — the score that stays after Twitch
- **Live** — stream as the synchronous layer; repo as what classrooms and future-you can fork

The show question: can a saved **Lively page** become a **yaml-jazz repo commit** without losing
liveness? What belongs in Automerge vs git? Trail:
[`yaml-jazz-collaboration-stack.md`](../../process/trails/yaml-jazz-collaboration-stack.md).

**What I'd love to talk about (your pick, any order)**

- **Pop-up menus ↔ pie menus** — you invented one cousin; I baked the other. Not a contest — a
  direct-manipulation retrospective with live demos. [`pie-menus-and-pop-ups.md`](pie-menus-and-pop-ups.md).
- **The live-object lineage** — BitBlt, Squeak-in-itself, **Fabrik**, the **Lively Kernel**, and
  the **Smalltalk Zoo** at CHM, Morphic, inspiring Snap!'s spectacular success. Walk one exhibit on air; change the system while it runs.
- **Where the browser lineage went** — Vanessa's **SqueakJS**, **Craig Latta**'s **Caffeine**
  (SqueakJS + WASM livecoding), and what that means for MicropolisCore + MOOLLM.
- **Bridges and round trips** — path expressions, query templates, JSONic APIs between a live
  scripting layer and a runtime. Same **JavaScript-everywhere / JSON message-passing** architecture
  Don used for **High Fidelity** distributed plugins (voting box, text chat) and **UnityJS** (2021
  notes — earlier cousin of this question).
- **Livelymerge ↔ MOOLLM** — see **Ink & Switch** section above; the collaboration-stack question
  Don most wants to think through with you.
