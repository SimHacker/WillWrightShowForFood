# In memoriam — Vanessa Freudenberg (d. 2025)

*Memorial mode: we represent and discuss her work and the stories friends tell; we do not interview her or speak as her.*
[Memorial standards](../../schemas/portrayal-standards.yml#memorial_mode)

Vanessa Freudenberg wrote **SqueakJS**, bringing a live Squeak Smalltalk virtual machine into
the web browser — the foundation that Craig Latta's **Caffeine** builds on, and a cornerstone of
the live-object, live-in-the-browser lineage this repo follows. She also contributed deeply to
Squeak/Etoys, the playful **Plopp/Plop** projects, and **Croquet / Multisynq**.

After SqueakJS, she had **sharp, insightful things to say about WebAssembly** — what WASM does and
doesn't buy a live Smalltalk VM, where JS and WASM each win, and how you'd translate a VM (and
individual methods) to WASM. That's exactly the thread **Craig Latta** carried forward with
Caffeine and Catalyst — so the right way to remember her is *with Craig*, livecoding the web she
helped make possible.

We remember her through the people who loved her — **Craig Latta** (anchor episode — Caffeine live),
**Dan Ingalls** (Smalltalk Zoo, SqueakJS credit), **Alan Kay** (constructionist microworld lineage),
and **Don Hopkins** (host). Warm invitations are going out; linked episodes or one arc — guests choose.
