## What Danielle has done

Danielle Bunten Berry (1949–1998) — game designer and a founding voice of multiplayer social gaming. At Ozark Softscape she created M.U.L.E. (1983), a four-player economic strategy game that became one of Electronic Arts' first and most beloved titles, and The Seven Cities of Gold (1984). She believed games were at their best when they brought people together in person, and she championed that idea for her whole career. Published as Dan Bunten before coming out as a trans woman in 1992. The Sims was dedicated to her.
