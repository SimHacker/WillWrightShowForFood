## What Scott has done

Scott Kim (b. 1955) — puzzle designer, visual artist, and computer scientist. A co-discoverer of ambigrams (with John Langdon; the term was coined by his friend Douglas Hofstadter), famous for his 1981 book *Inversions* — words lettered to read the same upside down or mirrored. His Stanford PhD (Computers and Graphic Design, 1987, advised by Donald Knuth) was *Viewpoint: Toward a Computer for Visual Thinkers* — a pixel-based text/graphics editor built on a UI principle he called 'visibility' (the screen's visual state IS the complete record). He designed the Silicon Graphics logo, was puzzlemaster for the game Heaven & Earth (1992, ~576 'Illusions' puzzles), and is a longtime puzzle columnist (*Games*, *Discover*, *Scientific American*) in the Martin Gardner recreational-math community.
