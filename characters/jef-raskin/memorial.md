# In memoriam — Jef Raskin (1943–2005)

*Memorial mode: we represent and discuss his work; we do not interview him or speak as him.*
[Memorial standards](../../schemas/portrayal-standards.yml#memorial_mode)

Jef Raskin started the **Macintosh** project at Apple and named it — then spent decades arguing that
what shipped was not the computer he meant. His life's work was the **humane interface**: not
"user-friendly" marketing, but design that **protects your locus of attention**, avoids arbitrary
memorization, and never traps you in modes you didn't choose.

His book *The Humane Interface* (2000) and the **Canon Cat** (1987) document appliance are the
anchors. **Archy** (The Humane Environment) was the unfinished shell his son **Aza Raskin** helped
carry forward. Terry Winograd hosted him at **CS547** for *Prolegomena to future interface design*
(2001) — one talk on a shelf of three hundred thirty-nine shoulders this repo inherits.

## Why this repo remembers him

We do **not** claim to be a Canon Cat. This show **is** a GitHub tree — yaml girders, verify CI,
inspectable guts. But every folder also carries a **`README.md`**: curated links, context, browse
order, invitation tone. GitHub's **directory listing** is brutalist view source; the **README** is
the humane overlay — same path, two projections. That is our synthesis of Raskin's goals with
**View Source** inspectability (OLPC, Repo Show RTFR): protect attention in the museum layer; drop
to listing when you need to prove provenance.

Read the framing in [`humane-interface-and-this-repo.md`](humane-interface-and-this-repo.md) and the policy in
[`process/character-colocation.yml`](../../process/character-colocation.yml) (`github_ux`).

## Show seed (if produced)

[**Remembering Jef Raskin**](../../repo-shows/remembering-jef-raskin.yml) is a **seed**, not a scheduled episode.
If Don ever produces it, he would ask living people with documented connections to Jef's work —
**after** consent, the same way any other guest is asked. `CHARACTER.yml` lists possible connections
(Terry hosted CS547; Ben, Bret, Alan, Walter have lineage ties; Don hosts). **None of that implies
they have agreed, been contacted, or owe us an appearance.**

Center the **intent**, not a feature laundry list — the same move Bret Victor asks for Engelbart:
*What world was Raskin trying to create?* A world where the interface never steals your train of
thought — and where you can still inspect the machinery when teaching or auditing matters.

We celebrate his documented public work; we do not speak as him.

*This is a collaborative memorial, not a signed work. Those who knew Jef — family, friends, and colleagues — are warmly invited to edit, correct, and add their own memories, documents, and stories whenever they're ready.*
