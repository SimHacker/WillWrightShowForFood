# Ideas to explore with Yoot Saito 👤

*Conversation hooks for a Repo Show — **Don's proposed topics**, each grounded in Yoot's
public games and his unique role as an interviewer of computing pioneers. Things Don would love
to follow **with** Yoot; not quotes, not claims about what he thinks.*
[Portrayal standards](../../schemas/portrayal-standards.yml) · invitation guest

## What Yoot has done

**Yoot Saito** — designer of **The Tower / SimTower**, **Seaman**, **Odama**; founder of
OPeNBooK and Vivarium. In the early 1990s he **personally interviewed Douglas Engelbart and Alan
Kay in Japan** (they visited together for a computer-magazine special) — primary-source oral
history, not fandom from the sidelines.

## Shared ground

Don helped **OCR-restore and annotate** Yoot's 1993 Alan Kay interview (name fixes:
McCafferty→Metcalfe, Engelbert→Engelbart, Cheatel→Cheadle; glossary of people and machines).
Alan Kay reviewed the transcript. Parallel thread: a **browser-native Yoot Tower** edition
(WASM/WebGL/Svelte) and Patrick Hulin's **tower-together** TypeScript reimplementation. See
[`correspondence.yml`](correspondence.yml).

## The hooks

### 1. The 1993 Alan Kay interview — on air with Alan
Screen the restored transcript. Yoot asks; Alan answers about **Engelbart's 1968 demo**, PARC,
the Alto, Smalltalk, **Ed Cheadle's Flex Machine**, Sketchpad, the LINC. Natural **Alan Kay**
and **Douglas Engelbart** memorial co-segments.

### 2. Where is the Engelbart interview?
Yoot also interviewed **Doug Engelbart** on the same Japan trip. Locate, restore, publish —
completing the pair Alan's transcript belongs to.

### 3. SimTower → SimCity → Micropolis — the elevator-game lineage
Yoot's tower simulations and Don's SimCity/Micropolis/OLPC work. Design patterns: vertical
**microworlds**, resource flow, emergent tenant behavior.

### 4. Yoot Tower in the browser — build session with Patrick Hulin
Steam edition exists; open web reimplementation in progress. Live compare: original, Patrick's
tick-for-tick TS, Don's WASM/Svelte sketch.

### 5. Seaman and Odama — weird interfaces as design research
Voice, time, and **uncanny** interaction — Yoot's non-tower work as HCI case studies (not
nostalgia clips).

### 6. Tower games as constructionist toys
No win state required — manage, observe, tell stories. Same philosophy as Will's **toys not
games** thread.
