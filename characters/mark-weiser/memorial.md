# In memoriam — Mark Weiser (1952–1999)

*Memorial mode: we represent and discuss his work; we do not interview him or speak as him.*
[Memorial standards](../../schemas/portrayal-standards.yml#memorial_mode)

Mark Weiser named the future we live in — **ubiquitous computing** — and argued for **calm
technology** that informs without demanding our attention. As CTO of Xerox PARC he shaped a
generation of research; his 1991 "The Computer for the 21st Century" still reads like a map.

For this show he is close to home: he **co-authored the CHI'88 empirical pie-menu study** with
Jack Callahan, **Don Hopkins**, and **Ben Shneiderman** — so we can remember him through the
people who sat at the table with him. (He also drummed for **Severe Tire Damage**, the first band
to play live over the Internet — calm technology, loud music.)

We celebrate his documented public work; we do not speak as him.

*This is a collaborative memorial, not a signed work. Those who knew Mark — family, friends, and colleagues — are warmly invited to edit, correct, and add their own memories, documents, and stories whenever they're ready.*
