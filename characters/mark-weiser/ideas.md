## What Mark has done

Mark Weiser (1952–1999) — father of ubiquitous computing and calm technology, and CTO of Xerox PARC. His 1991 'The Computer for the 21st Century' imagined computing woven invisibly into everyday life (tabs, pads, boards). He co-authored the CHI'88 empirical comparison of pie vs linear menus with Jack Callahan, Don Hopkins, and Ben Shneiderman — and drummed for Severe Tire Damage, the first band to play live on the Internet.
