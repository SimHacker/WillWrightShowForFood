## Why he's here

In **1978** Scott Adams wrote **Adventureland** on a TRS-80 — the first text adventure sold for
a home computer — and founded **Adventure International**. His real breakthrough was an
engineering one: a compact **data-driven adventure engine**. Each game was a *database* of
rooms, items, and verb/noun *actions*, run by a small portable interpreter, so one adventure
could ship across the TRS-80, Apple ][, Atari, VIC-20, and C64 — a whole world in ~16K with a
two-word parser. He later wrote the Marvel **Questprobe** adventures.

That "**compile the adventure to portable data + a tiny VM**" idea is the direct ancestor of
Don's **adventure compiler** and of Don's Terrapin **Logo Adventure**. Per Don, Scott wants to
build this *now*, is diving deeply into **AI**, and Don wants to develop the tooling for Scott
to use as well as himself — which makes for a perfect Repo Show: adventure, engines, and AI.
