## What Cynthia has done

Cynthia Solomon — one of the original designers of Logo (1967, at Bolt, Beranek & Newman, with Wally Feurzeig, Seymour Papert, Daniel G. Bobrow and Richard Grant; NSF-sponsored) and a lifelong pioneer of computing for children. She helped build the MIT Logo culture through the 1970s and worked at Atari Cambridge Research in the early 1980s on a children's computer (object-oriented Logo, gestural input, force feedback, music) alongside Alan Kay, Margaret Minsky, David Levitt and others. Author of *Computer Environments for Children: A Reflection on Theories of Learning and Education* (MIT Press, 1986) and co-author of the *History of Logo* (Proc. ACM HOPL, 2020). Honored with the Constructionism 2016 and FabLearn 2019 Lifetime Achievement Awards, and gave the inaugural Seymour Papert Memorial Lecture (CrossRoads 2018). On her YouTube channel she has shared a rare treasure trove of historical videos — Papert, Marvin and Margaret Minsky, kids programming turtles, and the Atari Cambridge lab.
