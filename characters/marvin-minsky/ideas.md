## What Marvin has done

Marvin Minsky (1927–2016) — one of the founding figures of artificial intelligence. With John McCarthy he co-founded the MIT AI Lab (1959), which he later co-directed with Seymour Papert. As a Princeton graduate student he built SNARC (1951), among the first neural-network learning machines. He developed frames (1974) and K-lines (1980) for representing knowledge and memory, and synthesized a lifetime of ideas in The Society of Mind (1986) and The Emotion Machine (2006). With Papert he wrote Perceptrons (1969). He invented the confocal scanning microscope, built early robotic arms, and advised Stanley Kubrick on 2001: A Space Odyssey. Turing Award, 1969; founding member of the MIT Media Lab. Husband of Gloria Rudisch; father of Margaret, Henry, and Juliana.
