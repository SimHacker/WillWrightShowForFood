🎛️ **Golan Levin** — Interactive/computational media artist, engineer, and educator (b. 1972, NYC; based in Pittsburgh). Professor of Electronic Art at Carnegie Mellon University (courtesy appointments in Design, Architecture, Computer Science, ETC) and former Director of CMU's Frank-Ratchye STUDIO for Creative Inquiry. MIT Media Lab alum; **Interval Research colleague of Don Hopkins and Paul Debevec**.

His work treats code as a creative material — gestural and audiovisual interfaces, generative systems, and critical making. Key works: the Audiovisual Environment Suite and the concert *Scribble*, *Messa di Voce*, *The Manual Input Workstation*, *Eyecode*, *Directrix*. Collected by MoMA, ZKM, and Cooper-Hewitt; shown at the Whitney Biennial and Ars Electronica. Co-author (with Tega Brain) of *Code as Creative Medium*. His archive lives at **flong.com** ("We fling flongs all day long").

Friend and colleague of Don. Proposed Repo Show guest — strong candidate for a **dual interview with Paul Debevec** about *Rouen Revisited* (their 1996 Interval photogrammetry project, the wood-and-brass kiosk) and what "truly and meaningfully interactive" can mean. His flong archive is, like Ken Perlin's Java demos, an archetype of the live, forkable, teaching-oriented work the Repo Show exists to celebrate and extend.
