## Who

**Norman Margolus** is a physicist and computer scientist — MIT PhD in physics (1987, under **Edward
Fredkin**) and a research affiliate at **MIT CSAIL** — known for foundational work on **cellular
automata** and **reversible computing**.

With **Tommaso Toffoli** he built the **CAM-6** cellular-automata machine and co-wrote
***Cellular Automata Machines: A New Environment for Modeling*** (MIT Press, 1987) — the book and
hardware that taught a generation how to actually *do* CA. He invented the **block cellular
automaton** and the **Margolus neighborhood**, built **reversible, universal** CA simulations of
**billiard-ball computers** (the **Critters** rule), and — with **Lev Levitin** — proved the
**Margolus–Levitin theorem**, a fundamental physical limit on how fast anything can compute. He
helped organize the seminal **1982 Physics & Computation** meeting, worked with **Tom Knight** on a
billiard-ball chip ("Flattop"), did early **reversible quantum gate** work, and founded the storage
company **Permabit**. His through-line: **computing is physics, and physics is computing** —
computronium and programmable matter.

## Why he's here

This one's personal to the toolset. Don built a **CAM6 emulator** — a CAM6-software-compatible
(per the *Cellular Automata Machines* book) **FORTH-programmable CAM orchestrator** and
**lookup-table rule compiler** on the Sun (NeWS + C), later ported all over. Norman and Toffoli's
**CAM-6** and book are literally the source that work follows. And it's not abstract: Norman is a
good friend of [**Henry Minsky**](../henry-minsky/README.md), and Don **met Norman in Boston** (visiting
Henry) and talked through this whole web — cellular automata, reversible computing, the physics of
computation. Henry is the natural bridge to invite him.

A show with Norman is a **cellular-automata summit**: run Don's CAM6 live, dig into block CA and the
Margolus neighborhood, play reversible rules forwards *and backwards*, and follow it all the way to
the physical limits of computation — ideally next to [Jim Crutchfield](../jim-crutchfield/README.md)'s edge
of chaos and [Scott Draves](../scott-draves/README.md)'s generative art.
