## Why these ideas fit

The 2006 Long Now talk *Playing with Time* (Eno + Will Wright) and the evening afterward (Don,
Brian, and Scott Draves with Electric Sheep glowing on every surface) were all circling one thing:
**you set up a system, define a few rules, and let it run** — the gardener, not the architect.
Twenty years on, AI generative tools finally make that idea cheap to build live on stream. So the
show is really: *generative systems across media — and what AI changes.*

## The hooks

### 1. Oblique Strategies as prompts for generative systems
Brian's **Oblique Strategies** (with Peter Schmidt) are lateral-thinking cards for breaking a
creative deadlock — "Honor thy error as a hidden intention," "Use an old idea," "Repetition is a
form of change." A live segment: treat the deck as a **prompt protocol** for an AI/generative
pipeline and watch how a constraint card bends an image or a piece of music in real time. The
card *is* the rule; the system fills in the rest.

### 2. Scenius ↔ collective intelligence (the Electric Sheep connection)
Brian coined **"scenius"** — genius as a property of a whole scene, not a lone individual. That is
*exactly* the engine of Scott Draves' **Electric Sheep**: thousands of people and machines vote
and breed the flock; beauty emerges from distributed decisions. A natural three-way thread with
Scott: scenius made literal, running live.

### 3. Generative across media
Ambient/generative **music** (*Music for Airports*, *Thursday Afternoon*, the *Bloom* app),
generative **visual art** (*77 Million Paintings*), evolutionary **images** (Electric Sheep), and
**simulation** (SimCity/Spore) are the same idea in different clothes. Map the family on stream;
build the smallest working example of each.

### 4. The 77 Million Paintings method, as a build
*77 Million Paintings* layers slowly shifting elements so the combination never repeats. A fun
live build: a layered generative composition (background field → structural form → motif →
atmosphere → a "happy accident" layer) where blend modes between layers produce emergence — a
toy any viewer can fork.

### 5. Art as trigger, not object
Brian's long-standing framing — *art as a trigger for experience rather than a fixed object* —
is the philosophy under the whole Repo Show idea: the repo isn't a finished artifact, it's a
**seed the audience builds on**. Worth talking through directly.

### 6. The gestural / musical pie menu (Don's idea to pitch)
Don's **pie menus** are direction-as-meaning. What if eight directions are eight notes/timbres and
the **gesture between nodes** (speed, curve) shapes the sound — a constraint-driven, improvisable
gestural instrument? Brian's whole career is "constraints create freedom," so this is a fun thing
to prototype together live. (Offered as a Don idea, not attributed to Brian.)

### 7. Taking Tiger Mountain, by transformation
Brian's *Taking Tiger Mountain (By Strategy)* (1974) was lovingly **re-recorded in full** by
**Doug Hilsinger & Caroleen Beatty** as *Taking Tiger Mountain Revisited* (2004) — cold experiment
reborn as warm rock. (Don actually crossed paths with that cover in San Francisco.) A lovely live
theme: honoring a work by **transforming** it, not copying it — the same move the Repo Show makes
with old code.

### 8. The Long Now lens
Brian coined **"The Long Now"** and helped found the Long Now Foundation. A grounding question for
the whole show: what do generative systems, open repos, and AI look like on a **10,000-year**
timescale — what erodes, what persists, what's worth seeding now?
