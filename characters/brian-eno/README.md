## Who

**Brian Eno** is a musician, composer, record producer, and visual artist. He pioneered
**ambient music** with *Ambient 1: Music for Airports* (1978) and has spent a career exploring
**generative** systems — music and art that grow, endlessly and without repeating, from a small
set of rules. His generative works include the *Bloom* app (with Peter Chilvers) and the visual
piece *77 Million Paintings*. As a producer he shaped landmark records by Roxy Music, David Bowie
(the Berlin trilogy), Talking Heads, Devo, and U2. With the painter Peter Schmidt he made the
*Oblique Strategies* deck of lateral-thinking prompts.

He coined the phrase **"The Long Now"** and is a founding board member of the **Long Now
Foundation** — where, in 2006, he and **Will Wright** gave the talk *Playing with Time*.

## Why a dream guest here

That 2006 talk is the hinge. Generative music and generative simulation are the same idea in
different media — grow a vast, surprising space from simple rules and let it run. After the talk,
Don introduced Brian to **Scott Draves**, and the three spent an evening at Scott's San Francisco
apartment bouncing ideas around, Electric Sheep glowing on every surface.

Twenty years later, with AI image and music generation finally catching up to what Eno, Draves,
and Wright were each circling, a reunion conversation almost writes itself: **rules, time,
surrender, and what AI changes** — across music, visual art, and simulation.
