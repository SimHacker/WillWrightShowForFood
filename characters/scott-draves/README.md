## Who

**Scott Draves** — "**Spot**" to his friends — is a software artist. In 1999 he started
**Electric Sheep**, a free, open-source screensaver that turns thousands of idle computers around
the world into one distributed render farm. The computers trade fragments of work over the
internet to compute abstract animations called **"sheep,"** and viewers vote for the ones they
like; a **genetic algorithm** breeds the favorites, mutating and crossing them so the flock
**evolves to please its global audience**. Scott calls it "the collective dream of a cyborg mind."
The name is an homage to Philip K. Dick's *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?*

The sheep are rendered with the **Fractal Flame algorithm**, which Scott invented and
**open-sourced in 1992** (flam3). Setting it free spread it everywhere: a Flame image appears on a
**Paul Simon** album cover and on the cover of Stephen Hawking's ***The Grand Design***, and the
**SIGGRAPH 2008 "Evolve"** conference built its whole graphic identity on Dream 191.14533.

His evolving-painting work, **Dreams in High Fidelity**, is a self-contained "painting that
evolves" — shown at **MoMA** (*Design and the Elastic Mind*, permanently hosted on MoMA.org),
**Google**'s lobby, the **Adler Planetarium**, and galleries from Pittsburgh to Moscow.

With his wife **Isabel Walcott Draves** he founded **Leaders in Software and Art (LSA)**, a
long-running salon and conference for tech artists. He later built the open-source **Beaker
Notebook** (a polyglot notebook for data science and visualization). In **2026** he relaunched the
Sheep as **Infinidream** — Generation 248, 1080p/60fps, with a phone remote and a generative-AI
platform, still open source.

He studied under **two legendary Andys**: at **Brown University** (BS Mathematics, 1990) in **Andy
van Dam's** graphics lab — where his thesis, **`fnord`** (a functional language for higher-dimensional
geometry, co-written with **Nick Thompson**), became the workhorse for **Thomas Banchoff's** 4D
artwork — and at **Carnegie Mellon University** (PhD, 1997; thesis *Automatic Program Specialization
for Interactive Media* — advised by **Andy Witkin** & Dana Scott early on, then **Peter Lee**).
Alongside the art runs a deep engineering career — Transmeta, DreamWorks (*Shrek 2*), Google, Two
Sigma (Beaker Notebook), CTRL-labs/Meta (neuromotor interfaces), and Stability AI.

## Why a Repo Show

Don and Scott go back to **the Bay Area in the early 1990s** — most likely through Don's housemate
**Nick Thompson**, who knew Spot from Brown (they co-wrote `fnord`) and was then at **SGI** building
Open Inventor. Don, at Sun, ran Scott's early Fractal Flame work on his SparcStation II and talked
interactive performance-art graphics while building his CAM6 cellular-automata platform. They
reconnected at **Carnegie Mellon** (Scott in his CS PhD, Don with Brad Myers doing Lisp UI and
porting SimCity from NeWS to Tcl/Tk), and have stayed friends ever since — recently teaming up again
on a cross-platform **Electric Sheep / Unity** client. *(The Nick-Thompson bridge and earliest dates
are Don's recollection, to confirm with Scott when he reviews this page. Scott himself used SGI
machines as an intern in Tokyo in 1991 and at NTT-Data in 1993 — Nick was the SGI employee.)*

There's a perfect thread here: **generative and evolutionary art** — from the **Fractal Flame**
math, through the distributed, vote-bred **Electric Sheep**, to today's **AI image generation** in
**Infinidream**. It's the same question Don, Will Wright, and Brian Eno were circling in 2006:
how do you grow beauty from simple rules and a crowd of minds and machines? Read the old Flame
papers, run the Sheep live, and riff on where it goes next.

## The Brian Eno evening (2006)

In June 2006, after the **Long Now Foundation** talk *Playing with Time* by **Brian Eno and Will
Wright**, Don introduced **Brian Eno** to Scott. The three met at Scott's San Francisco apartment
and bounced ideas around; Brian saw Electric Sheep and the hour-per-frame HD *Dreams in High
Fidelity*. (Scott photographed the evening — Brian with Electric Sheep "on his head.") That night
is why **Brian Eno** is a [dream guest](../brian-eno/) here, ideally reachable through **Will** and
**Scott**.
