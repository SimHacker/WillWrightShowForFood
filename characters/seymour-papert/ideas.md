## What Seymour has done

Seymour Papert (1928–2016) — South-African-born mathematician, computer scientist, and educator who reshaped how we think about children and computers. After working with Jean Piaget in Geneva, he came to MIT and co-founded the AI Lab with Marvin Minsky. He co-created Logo (1967, with Wally Feurzeig and Cynthia Solomon) — the language of the turtle — and coined constructionism: the idea that people learn best by *building* things they care about and can share. His Mindstorms (1980) is a foundational text of educational computing; LEGO Mindstorms is named after it. His influence runs straight through Scratch, One Laptop Per Child, and this project's own constructionist mission.
