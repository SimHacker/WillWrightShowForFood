# In memoriam — Seymour Papert (1928–2016)

*Memorial mode: we represent and discuss his documented work; we do not interview him or speak as
him, and we invent no quotes or inner life.*
[Memorial standards](../../schemas/portrayal-standards.yml#memorial_mode)

Seymour Papert changed the question from *"what can computers teach children?"* to *"what can
children make with computers?"* Born in Pretoria and shaped as an anti-apartheid activist, he worked
with **[Jean Piaget](../jean-piaget/README.md)** in Geneva — the single largest influence on his thinking —
then came to MIT and **co-founded the AI Lab with [Marvin Minsky](../marvin-minsky/README.md)**. With Wally
Feurzeig and Cynthia Solomon he co-created **Logo** (1967) and its **turtle**: a way for children to
*program the computer, not be programmed by it*.

He named and championed **constructionism** — the idea that we learn most deeply by building things
that matter to us and that we can share with others — and laid it out in **Mindstorms: Children,
Computers, and Powerful Ideas** (1980), one of the foundational books of educational computing.
**LEGO Mindstorms** is named after it. His line runs straight through **Scratch** (Mitchel
Resnick's Lifelong Kindergarten group), **One Laptop Per Child**, and the constructionist mission of
*this* project — open, playful, build-to-learn Micropolis.

We remember him by inviting the people who carry his work forward: **[Walter Bender](../walter-bender/README.md)**
(OLPC), **[Brian Harvey](../brian-harvey/README.md)** (Logo, BJC), **[Ken Kahn](../ken-kahn/README.md)** (ToonTalk),
**[Jens Mönig](../jens-monig/README.md)** (Snap!), and **[Alan Kay](../alan-kay/README.md)**.

We celebrate his documented public work; we do not speak as him.

**Lineage:** [Jean Piaget](../jean-piaget/README.md) → Papert → Logo / constructionism → the show's learning
spine. See the [constructionist-lineage trail](../../process/trails/constructionist-lineage.md).

*This is a collaborative memorial, not a signed work. Those who knew Seymour — family, friends, and colleagues — are warmly invited to edit, correct, and add their own memories, documents, and stories whenever they're ready.*
